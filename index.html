About Us

Code the Change at Berkeley is an organization that empowers computer scientists and engineers to use their technical skills for social good through non-profit projects.

We form project teams to assist non-profits in achieving their goals, whether it be through web development or mobile app design. Our project teams are comprised of Cal students with a passion for both technology and the social sector. We believe that as long as you’re willing to learn, you’re welcome to join us in our project teams where you can develop your technical abilities and form meaningful relationships with members who share similar interests.

Code the Change has active chapters on both the UC Berkeley and Stanford campuses. You can learn more about the Stanford chapter here.

Projects

Past Projects

Meerkat

Meerkat is working to provide a platform to facilitate partnerships between nonprofits and businesses, enabling nonprofits to fundraise and businesses to advertise. We helped them implement their login system as well as their profile pages.


GLOW Foundation

Founded in 2006, GLOW’s mission is to build the capacity of schools and nonprofits to help empower under-resourced students and their families to overcome barriers to higher education access and success. We helped them create a mobile app on the Android platform so that they could easily communicate with and aid their students. www.glowfoundation.org


Asian-Americans for Community Involvement

The mission of Asian Americans for Community Involvement is to improve the health, mental health and well-being of individuals, families and the Asian community. We worked on a web app that allows hospitals to post appointments for patients who need help getting to their appointments. www.aaci.org
